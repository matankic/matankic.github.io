Abstract

Conventional cameras cannot see through opaque container walls to determine fill levels. We propose a speckle-based vibration sensing system that captures millimeter-scale surface vibrations at multiple points on sealed containers. By illuminating containers with a laser grid and capturing high-speed speckle shifts, we record multi-point vibration signals elicited by acoustic excitation. We then introduce Vibration-Transformer, a novel transformer-based architecture that processes the spectral vibration responses to jointly classify container type and infer hidden liquid levels. Our system generalizes across unseen speaker positions, container instances, and ambient noises, achieving 2â€“9% mean absolute error in within- and out-of-distribution tests. This approach facilitates non-contact monitoring of liquid levels in challenging settings such as heavy, hazardous, or hard-to-reach containers.

System Overview

The captured two-axis speckle shifts at three points per container are converted to magnitude spectra via FFT and tokenized. Our Vibration-Transformer applies a shared PointTransformer per point and a ShapeTransformer to fuse multi-point features, outputting container class and fill-level predictions.

Experimental Setup

Our experimental rig includes a defocused camera capturing speckle patterns of a laser-split 2D grid on container surfaces, six speakers for acoustic excitation, and scales for ground-truth fill measurement.

Results

Within-distribution testing yields 2% MAE in fill-level inference. Unseen-instance tests achieve 9% MAE, and ambient-sound scenarios maintain high accuracy across different container types and acoustic conditions.

Conclusion

We introduce a first-of-its-kind non-contact method for inferring hidden liquid levels using speckle vibrometry and transformer-based analysis. Our approach opens pathways for remote monitoring of containers in industrial, hazardous, and logistics applications, demonstrating robust performance across diverse conditions and container types.
